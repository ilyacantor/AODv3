A) Add a “Risk Case” aggregation layer (this is the big lever)

Emit raw triggers as you do today, but the KPI and default UI should use:

security_risk_cases_total

plus a breakdown by type:

identity_gap_cases

finance_gap_cases

data_conflict_cases

Then show “raw triggers” only in drill-down.

B) Add confidence + materiality fields to each emitted risk

Every risk should carry:

confidence: HIGH | MED | LOW

materiality: HIGH | MED | LOW

triage_priority: derived (e.g., P0/P1/P2)

Default KPI = count of cases where:

triage_priority in (P0, P1)
not “all cases”.

This keeps your “security risks” headline honest.

C) Tighten the trigger gates per finding type (pragmatic defaults)
IDENTITY_GAP (HIGH)

Keep it strict and defensible:
Trigger only if:

asset has strong activity evidence (any of):

cloud evidence (resource/API calls)

finance recurring contract OR recurring transactions

audit-log/SSO-like evidence (if present)

AND idp_present == false (no SSO/SCIM governance)

Optional but powerful:

require 2 independent planes (e.g., discovery + finance OR discovery + cloud) to call it “actionable”.

FINANCE_GAP (HIGH)

Trigger only if:

recurring spend or a contract exists

AND spend over threshold (per month or annualized), e.g.:

>= $200/month OR >= $2,000/year (pick what matches your ICP)

AND no governance visibility (no CMDB owner, no IdP app, etc.)

This alone usually cuts FINANCE_GAP by 70–90%.

DATA_CONFLICT (MEDIUM)

Trigger only if:

conflict is on a security-relevant field (you can start with a small whitelist):

owner / business_owner

environment (prod/staging)

data classification

auth/governance state

AND conflict persists across trusted planes (CMDB vs IdP vs Cloud)
(avoid “discovery guessed X” causing conflicts)

Also: dedupe by (asset, field_name).

How the UI should present this (so it doesn’t look insane)

Top-level tile should be:

“Security Risks (Actionable)” = P0+P1 case count

Under it, show:

P0: “Immediate” (e.g., Identity gaps with high confidence + meaningful activity)

P1: “High priority”

P2: “Backlog / monitor”

And a second, smaller stat:

“All security risk cases” (includes P2)

optionally “raw triggers” hidden behind tooltip

This preserves your “standalone KPI” concept without panicking users.
