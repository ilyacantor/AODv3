Here’s a **clean, enterprise-grade categorization of security risks** that fits AOD’s philosophy (evidence-driven, low-noise, actionable) and scales without becoming SOC theater.

I’ll give you:

1. The **top-level categories** (what CISOs expect)
2. **How your existing findings map**
3. What’s **intentionally excluded**
4. How this drives UI, KPIs, and prioritization

---

## 1. Canonical Security Risk Categories (AOD-aligned)

### **1) Identity & Access Risks**

> *“Who can access this, and should they?”*

**Definition**
Risks where access exists or is implied **without centralized identity control or policy enforcement**.

**Examples**

* App in use with no IdP / SSO / SCIM
* Orphaned access paths (API keys, local auth, service accounts)
* Access exists but ownership is unknown or unmanaged

**AOD Mapping**

* `IDENTITY_GAP` → **Identity & Access Risk (HIGH)**

**Why it’s first**

* Most breaches start here
* Clean, defensible, board-level understandable
* Almost always actionable

---

### **2) Financially-Backed Shadow IT Risks**

> *“We are paying for something we do not govern.”*

**Definition**
Spend or contractual obligation exists **without corresponding governance or visibility**, implying unsanctioned systems with unknown access, data handling, or compliance posture.

**Examples**

* SaaS contract with no CMDB entry and no IdP integration
* Recurring charges tied to unknown vendor/product
* Department-level tools bypassing IT/security review

**AOD Mapping**

* `FINANCE_GAP` → **Shadow IT Risk (HIGH)**
  *(only when spend is recurring + material)*

**Key nuance**
This is **not “finance hygiene”** — money + usage = latent security exposure.

---

### **3) Data Integrity & Control Risks**

> *“We can’t trust what we think we know.”*

**Definition**
Conflicting or inconsistent authoritative data across systems of record that undermines security posture, enforcement, or auditability.

**Examples**

* CMDB says “prod” while cloud says “staging”
* Owner differs across IdP vs CMDB
* Asset marked “inactive” but shows active usage

**AOD Mapping**

* `DATA_CONFLICT` → **Data Integrity Risk (MEDIUM)**

**Why it matters**
Bad data leads to:

* Missed access reviews
* Incorrect risk acceptance
* Broken controls

Usually **not P0**, but dangerous if ignored.

---

### **4) Coverage & Visibility Gaps (Conditional Security Risk)**

> *“We can’t protect what we can’t see.”*

**Definition**
Assets that are active but **outside one or more core control planes**, reducing the organization’s ability to enforce security policies.

**Examples**

* Asset exists but not in CMDB *and* has access or spend
* System used in production but not logged centrally
* SaaS detected in discovery but invisible to security tooling

**AOD Mapping**

* Derived from combinations:

  * `CMDB_GAP` + activity
  * `IDENTITY_GAP` + usage
  * `FINANCE_GAP` + no ownership

**Important**
This category should **not always count as a security risk** — only when paired with evidence of use or impact.

---

### **5) Exposure Amplifiers (Secondary / Contextual)**

> *“This makes other risks worse.”*

**Definition**
Conditions that **increase blast radius** or reduce response effectiveness but are rarely standalone vulnerabilities.

**Examples**

* No owner assigned
* Duplicate representations of same asset
* Ambiguous vendor/product identity

**AOD Mapping**

* `GOVERNANCE_GAP`
* `DUPLICATION_RISK`

**Treatment**

* Never P0 on their own
* Used as **risk multipliers**, not headline counts

| `IDENTITY_GAP`     | Identity & Access  | ✅ Yes               | Core security risk         |

| `DATA_CONFLICT`    | Data Integrity     | ⚠️ Sometimes        | Field-scoped               |

| `GOVERNANCE_GAP`   | Exposure Amplifier | ❌ No                | Hygiene                    |
| `DUPLICATION_RISK` | Exposure Amplifier | ❌ No                | Context only               |

This preserves your taxonomy **without re-labeling everything “security.”**

---

## 3. What AOD Should Explicitly NOT Call a Security Risk

These should *never* inflate the “Security Risks” KPI:

* Missing CMDB entries **with no usage**
* One-time or trivial spend
* Discovery-only signals (DNS, passive observation)
* Duplicate records without access implications
* Unknown vendor **without activity**

This distinction is what separates AOD from noisy CNAPP/iPaaS/SaaS-security tools.

## 4. How This Should Drive the UI + KPIs

### Top-Level KPI

**“Actionable Security Risks”**

* Count of **risk cases**
* Only P0 + P1
* Deduped per asset + category

### Breakdown (stacked or drill-down)

* Identity & Access
* Shadow IT (Financial)
* Data Integrity

### Secondary KPIs

* “Visibility Gaps”
* “Governance Hygiene Issues”

This reinforces:

> *Security ≠ cleanliness ≠ completeness*
