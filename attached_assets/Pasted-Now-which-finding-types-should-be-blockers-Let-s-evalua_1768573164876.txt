Now: which finding types should be blockers?

Let’s evaluate each one strictly through the lens of “safe to connect?”

1️⃣ identity_gap → BLOCKING (default)

Why

AAM will create credentials, tokens, or integrations

Without IdP / SSO, you have:

no lifecycle control

no offboarding

no access auditability

This is non-negotiable in most enterprises.

Decision

✅ BLOCK AAM connection by default

User-facing explanation

“This system cannot be connected until identity governance is established.”

Override allowed?

Yes — customer can downgrade to “warn only”.

2️⃣ finance_gap → BLOCKING (default)

This is subtle but important.

Why

AAM creates operational dependency

Connecting systems with unknown ownership or spend is dangerous

Enterprises will require cost accountability before enabling integrations

Decision

✅ BLOCK AAM connection by default

Human explanation

“We detected active spend without an accountable owner.
Connection is paused to prevent unmanaged dependencies.”

Overrideable?
Yes — some customers may allow “connect first, govern later”.

3️⃣ cmdb_gap → NON-BLOCKING (default)

Why

CMDB hygiene is often poor

Blocking on CMDB would stall adoption

CMDB completeness is usually downstream of discovery

Decision

❌ Not blocking by default

Still important, but informational / hygiene.

4️⃣ governance_gap → NON-BLOCKING (default)

Depends on what you mean by governance.

Your example:

“Notion lacks defined data classification”

This is important, but:

Often undefined even in mature orgs

Hard to enforce automatically

Blocking here would cause friction

Decision

❌ Not blocking by default
(But can be upgraded to blocking in “High-control” preset)

5️⃣ data_conflict → BLOCKING (default)

This one is critical and easy to underestimate.

Why

Conflicting owners / environments / classifications means:

wrong credentials

wrong scopes

wrong data exposure

AAM must know which truth to act on

Decision

✅ BLOCK AAM connection by default

Explanation

“We cannot safely connect this system because sources disagree on its identity or ownership.”

This is a safety block, not a policy block.

6️⃣ duplication_risk → NON-BLOCKING (default)

Why

Dupes are common and often benign

Blocking would cause unnecessary friction

AAM can often still connect once one canonical asset is chosen

Decision

❌ Not blocking by default
