Reframe the colors (lock this in)
🔴 Red — Cannot Connect

AAM must not connect

Unsafe or undefined to proceed

Objective prerequisites missing

Examples:

Identity gap (no SSO)

Finance gap (unowned spend)

Data conflict (conflicting ownership / env)

These are hard gates.

🟡 Yellow — Judgment Required

AAM could connect

But the system cannot decide confidently

Requires human context or prioritization

Yellow answers:

“Is this worth acting on, and how?”

🟢 Green — Informational

Hygiene only

No impact on connection

Safe to ignore or batch later

So what belongs in Yellow?
✅ Zombies — Yes

Zombies are the canonical yellow case:

Registered

No recent activity

Might be waste, might be seasonal

Pure human judgment.

✅ Duplication Risk — Yes

Potential overlap

Often benign

Needs confirmation, not enforcement

✅ Ambiguous Classification (subset of data_conflict)

This is important:

If data_conflict is:

Hard contradiction → 🔴 Red
(e.g. prod vs dev, different owners)

Soft ambiguity → 🟡 Yellow
(e.g. SaaS vs internal tool, vendor vs product)

So data_conflict splits.

❌ CMDB Gap — No (Green)

Known hygiene issue

Not a decision blocker

Everyone has this problem

❌ Governance Gap — No (Green by default)

Unless customer explicitly elevates it.

The test (use this every time)

Ask one question:

“Would a reasonable engineer feel unsafe wiring this into production?”

Yes → 🔴

No, but unsure → 🟡

No, and who cares → 🟢

That’s the rule. No exceptions.

Why this matters

If Yellow becomes “everything that’s not green,” it loses meaning.
If Yellow becomes “only zombies,” you underuse it.

Yellow is human discernment, not decay.
