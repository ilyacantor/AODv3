## Why infrastructure domains are excluded (the *real* reason)

Infrastructure domains are excluded **not because they’re unimportant**, but because **they are not enterprise-owned assets** in the sense AOD is trying to model.

There are three concrete reasons:

### 1. Shared multi-tenant infrastructure ≠ enterprise asset

Domains like:

* `cloudfront.net`
* `gstatic.com`
* `awsstatic.com`

are **shared delivery planes** used by *thousands of unrelated applications*.

If you see traffic to `cloudfront.net`, that does **not** mean:

> “The enterprise uses CloudFront as an application.”

It means:

> “Some application loaded a static file.”

Treating these as assets causes:

* massive catalog explosion
* meaningless shadow/zombie classifications
* governance signals that are impossible to interpret

So exclusion is a **modeling choice**, not a shortcut.

---

### 2. Corporate roots and portals are not deployable assets

Domains like:

* `microsoft.com`
* `office.com`

are:

* vendor homepages
* generic login portals
* not tenant-specific
* not deployable, owned, or configurable by the enterprise

They act as **front doors**, not systems.

Cataloging them produces:

* false governance certainty
* shadow noise
* broken asset counts

---

### 3. These domains destroy determinism if included

If infrastructure domains are allowed to behave like assets:

* They appear everywhere
* They always look “active”
* They always look “governed” via vendor propagation
* They swamp meaningful signals

Your invariant:

> **Classification must be deterministic**

is violated if these are included without special handling.

---

## Should this be policy-driven?

**Yes. Unequivocally.**

Excluding infrastructure is not a technical rule — it is **product intent**.

Therefore:

* ❌ Hard-coded bans are wrong

* ❌ Silent drops are wrong

* ❌ “Magic lists” are wrong

* ✅ Explicit policy

* ✅ Explainable behavior

* ✅ Assessable alignment with Farm

## How it should exist in policy (semantically correct)

Not “banned domains.”

Instead:

### Policy concept: *Infrastructure Domain Handling*

```json
"infrastructure_domain_handling": {
  "mode": "exclude", 
  "shared_infrastructure_domains": [
    "cloudfront.net",
    "gstatic.com",
    "awsstatic.com"
  ],
  "vendor_root_domains": [
    "microsoft.com",
    "office.com"
  ],
  "classification_behavior": "exclude"
}
```

Possible modes:

* `exclude` → never catalog, never classify
* `observe_only` → visible but never governed, never zombie/shadow
* `shadow` → allowed but always shadow

This makes the intent explicit.
