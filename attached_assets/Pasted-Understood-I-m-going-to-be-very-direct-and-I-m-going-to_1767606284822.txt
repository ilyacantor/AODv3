## 3. The Correct Fix: Late-Binding Identity (Not Another Refactor)

### What we do NOT touch

* Normalization
* Correlation
* Entity IDs
* Evidence generation
* Admission logic semantics

### What we DO change

Only **asset identity finalization at persistence time**.

#### Concrete behavior

At the moment we create an `Asset`:

1. **If a canonical domain exists → use it as `asset.name`**
2. **If not → fall back to original normalized name**
3. **Always preserve:**

   * original_name
   * all observed domains
   * all correlated domains

No guillotine. No rejection. No mid-stream mutation.

**Impact:**

* Zombies immediately recover (Farm can now match)
* Shadows stay intact
* Zero regression risk upstream
* Deterministic behavior preserved

## 4. Fan-In Is Real — Treat It Explicitly

You *will* have cases where:

* `foo.ai`
* `foo.com`
* `login.foo.com`

All resolve to the same registered domain.

### The rule

> **Fan-in is merged, not rejected.**

### Minimal merge strategy (safe and sufficient)

* Keep the **oldest entity_id** as canonical
* Union:

  * evidence_refs
  * observations
  * finance records
* Recompute findings once

Do **not** invent heuristics here yet.

**Impact:**

* No UUID collisions
* No dropped assets
* Predictable merges
* Future ML/heuristics possible later
