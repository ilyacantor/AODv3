Yep — **this is the kind of “broader + evil” that explains why fixes feel like they don’t land.** And based on what you just found, there are **two separate evils**:

## Evil #1: Ghost assets (catalog contains things that shouldn’t exist)

If your invariant check found **17 cataloged assets with no valid admission basis**, that’s not a “bug in one gate.” That’s a **pipeline integrity breach**: somewhere you’re allowing assets to end up in `cataloged` even when *no gate is true*.

This is usually caused by one (or more) of:

* **Run-scope leakage**: assets from prior runs/snapshots still in the working set and getting re-cataloged without fresh evidence.
* **Merge contamination**: asset aggregation unions “something” (refs, lens flags, anchors) even when the admitting evidence doesn’t survive.
* **Cataloging step not re-checking admission**: i.e., catalog stage trusts “admitted earlier” or “has refs” instead of recomputing “passes a gate.”

**Non-negotiable invariant (make it fatal):**

> `cataloged_asset => admitted_asset => (idp_gate OR cmdb_gate OR cloud_gate OR finance_gate OR discovery_gate)`

Not “has evidence_refs”, not “has anchors”, not “has lens coverage”, not “has name”.

If that invariant fails, you don’t have a “classification bug.” You have a **system-of-record bug**.

## Evil #2: The 6 IdP assets are *actually* HAS_IDP in AOD

This is the opposite of what we thought earlier, and it’s the real blocker:

* AOD reconciliation API returns **HAS_IDP**
* Stored `lens_coverage.idp = True`
* Farm expects **NO_IDP**

That means the disagreement is **semantic**, not formatting.

### The key fork in the road

Only two possibilities exist:

### A) **Farm snapshot truly has NO IdP record for those domains**

Then AOD is pulling IdP correlation from somewhere else:

* stale cached IdP plane
* cross-run / cross-snapshot pollution
* wrong tenant/snapshot binding
* “policy center” points to one snapshot, but correlation uses another

That’s *evil* and aligns with your ghost-assets finding.

### B) **Farm snapshot *does* have IdP records for those domains**

Then Farm’s “NO_IDP expected” is wrong — meaning:

* Farm’s reason-code generator applies **extra suppression rules** (e.g., “if asset name has (Legacy)/-prod then treat as NO_IDP even if domain matches”)
* Or Farm is correlating differently than policy implies

Either way: **Farm/AOD are not using the same correlation rules or the same data.**
